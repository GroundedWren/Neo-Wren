What Water Wants

By Vera, December 6, 2025

Hopping twigs in scraggled brush I feel the air of flight Talons numb to sacred ground Eyes on clouds awash in light

The warmth is on and in my face A soaking brain, a trembling core What water wants, away away Seeking cup, rapport, amour

To learn, to know, to know to learn To thumb and trace, to mark and note And lose control, mal-winged soul Gaze for graze on bare of throat

Kiss the leaves and they return Gold and green in songs you sing So to feel is to be touched Water wrung in wring by wring

Dampened tongues and perspiration To and fro in tidal lock Valley snakes and great salt lakes Fingers, flow, and flooded dock

Morning mist and tilled dark dirt Tattered trees in vibrant bloom Our sky's been kissing on its ground Rings this joyful bird in tune
