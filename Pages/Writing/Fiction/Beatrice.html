Beatrice

By Vera, January 12, 2026

Captain Marlynn pitied the ragged Tiefling before her - losing your ship is the worst tragedy that may befall a captain, never mind that losing your crew meant you had to endure weeks, or even months, of sifting through mediocre seamen to find enough talent to keep your sails raised. She'd begun to absently mull over the blessing it had been to inherit Captain Cooder's ship and most of her crew when the word Sambar snapped her back into the conversation.

It shouldn't be far out of the way, going to Ashvahan you'll be in the Sambar Run anyway, and I'm sure that the Mountainside Training Company would pay well for my, uh, she paused and squeezed the fingers on her left hand as they continued to bleed through their makeshift cloth bandage, relatively safe return.

Marlynn couldn't help grinning. Ah, Sambar! Yes, we'll be swinging right by it - I'm sure that we could, she hesitated a moment, remembering the hearty bag of gold she'd been given for an expedient voyage to Ashvahan. Shit. Cindra might not go for this, but then again Marlynn was the captain and she didn't take orders on her ship, gods dammit! She had earned that. We can make it work, darling, she concluded decisively. No fancy little Dracai was going to get between her and an opportunity to see Beatrice again.

There was a certain pattern that Marlynn had with her girlfriends, a certain rhythm her love life had fallen into. She would sail into port, woo the prettiest girl at the local pub with her scoundrel's charm and tales of plunder on the high seas, lose herself in a night of wild passion, and then flee in the morning with pockets full of ill-gotten souvenirs with a paramour in hot pursuit. It seemed though that no matter how dramatic Marlynn's exits were, her (usually armed) flings chased her in retreat at least in part because they wanted more. And when tempers had cooled with a little time, she would let them punish her for her transgressions and the passionate cycle would begin anew.

Beatrice was... different. It had started out similarly enough; she bought a round for the house at The Sunken Sump to celebrate a successful haul and caught a long glance from across the dimly-lit room. It was cold in the Undercity and Marlynn was still wearing a freezing rain in her hair, but the very sight of that dark-skinned gnome warmed her in a way not even the strongest rum could. She was absolutely striking in her fine cloth and lace, her cut jewels and her gold rings. Sambar's undercity is dangerous - for a beautiful stout woman to wear such lavish things amongst the riff-raff, and with such confidence, was the ultimate flex of power. It said one thing: Do. Not. Fuck with me. And it turned her on.

Beatrice saw right through her. She brushed Marlynn's scoundrel swagger aside as easily as she flipped her long, black hair over her shoulder. After only a few minutes of talking she had the tall, proud, braggadocious Marlynn gently but firmly pinned by her exacting questions, bare and blushing before her piercing green eyes. Marlynn was, at last, seen for who she was: a damaged and furious little girl; a raw and frightened woman; a dreamer playing the hedonist. It was... a relief. None have known her so well since her mother was killed, and dropping the act was a long overdue exhale. Beatrice didn't take her breath away, she gave it back.

Their... whatever it was, broke the pattern. When Marlynn came to Sambar she would begin her usual routine with Beatrice, but only begin. They went to plays. They sipped wine. They cuddled by the hearth. And never, despite Beatrice's obvious wealth, did Marlynn take anything. She was falling in love, and it was terrifying.

The morning she did take something, there was no chase, no screaming pursuit, and no theatrics. Marlynn didn't like it - this wasn't the script, and it didn't feel like Beatrice, either. Why hadn't she given chase? Surely she was expecting this? Marlynn was a smuggler, a scoundrel, a thief who fucks with you as she fucks with you - that was all she could be, and Beatrice had to have seen that with her boundless insight. But....

Marlynn fiddled with the clockwork device in her hands: an expensive-looking, heavy thing. It tired her to hold for long; the acid in her forearms and the saltwater behind her eyes sloshed around, burning and stinging as she found herself short of breath. She had to see her again, to lie in her arms, to squirm in her gaze, to writhe in her bed. She had to fix this.

They were going to Sambar.
