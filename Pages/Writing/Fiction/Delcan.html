Delcan's Personal Journal

1st Hammer, 1594 DR

A new year breaks in Gond's glory, a fresh canvas upon which to paint our wondrous works in his name and hang them high for at all to marvel. This stark white sunrise over the shimmering, frozen sea does beg the question, though, of what the great ships to the South will do once the ice breaks and they may once more comingle - will they paint this wonderful snow red? Nyx is a tricky one, as full of hot air as fire in my estimation, but hot air begets pressure, and pressure begets volatility. The indomitable Woman King of Chult is unlikely to allow for an incremental de-escalation, especially now that there's been a Chultan death at sea in the skirmishes.

There are some in the Church who see this as an opportunity - conflict is indeed one of the most prodigious mothers of invention - but surely death cannot be glorious in the eyes of Gond? Each lost soul is one creature who can no longer marvel at creation; He would surely oppose this.

There are some who would disagree, insisting that death by a work of Gond is the highest form of worship to him. What a wonderful offering it makes for the visage of His creation to be the last touch one ever knows, their bloody body baptized with holy wounds wrought by his brilliance as a final rite. It does have a poetry to it, and Jelxis has a way of making it sound beautiful.

One way or another, a new dawn breaks over Lanthan. Glory to Gond.

7th Flamerule, 1594 DR

It's finally come - war! We've just received word that Vushire on the jungle nation's South coast was razed in brutal fashion by mighty dragonborn soldiers brandishing their black steel. We've already had a Petition for Massive Requisition submitted by King Tigin herself, and, Gond help me, I'm going to grant it. It's just a matter of time until the Nyx does the same, and a million more opportunists flood our docks looking for mechanical war machines.

Jelxis seemed so pleased at the last meeting of Parliament - it worries me that a Master Seeker cannot taste bitterness in the blood spilled by Gond's wonderful works. I'm glad to have Corad in these meetings as his perspective is so much like mine; it really does reassure me that I've not gone completely blind to the wondrous nature of Gond (as Jelxis seems to imply from time to time). Writing that, I realize she's coming awfully close to blasphemy. I admire her spirit, but I do also get the sense that she's dangerous.

23rd Elasis, 1594 DR

Another excommunication today. The public forums we open to discuss Petitions for Large Requisitions have only gotten more heated as time's gone on. Since that one young Seeker (Rasten?) made such a show a month ago every public meeting turns into a shouting match. Can they not see that this is all for the Glory of Gond? That the infusion of fresh vigor into our inventors over the last months has been the greatest we've seen in a generation? Yes, we mourn the dead, but Gond makes no mistakes; every invention is holy.

Glory to Gond, I do still wish it to be over. Corad's taken my confession again recently, and I can't help but feel that I've come to depend on him more and more. He's a kind man whose mind is a wondrous work of its own. Not to mention that figure of his. (Gods, I pray no one finds this journal.)

1st Hammer, 1595 DR

It's ended, though we'll see for how long. Blackroot was destroyed, and the Chultans seem satisfied in their position for the time being. Jelxis is disappointed, but I'm just glad we can return our hands to works that don't spill blood.

A new dawn breaks again over our shimmering, icy sea. I hope for the future, despite everything. The wheels of the machine spin on, may our work please Him and brighten the world for our siblings.

11th Alturiak, 1596 DR

Corad's sought council again on a project of his, though Gond knows he needs it not; he and his students are building truly wondrous works. His employment of Gondian crystals is particularly fascinating - so efficient, so elegant. I have seen Archmage Zinnoa's signature on some of the schematics, but I know my friend; it's all him.

It is refreshing to advise on projects that are not designed to end life. It'll never be as bad as during the Waigeo war, but we see far more weapons being constructed these days than we ever used to. It is beginning to embitter me, despite the council of Jelxis. She's taken my confession several times on the matter and remains ardent as ever that we were doing as Gond would have wanted during the war, and that each weapon we make now is just another tool in his Glory.

It's taken me a long time, but Gond has revealed to me that this is not in his vision. I don't know how much longer I can support such things, much as most of Lanthan will resent me for this stance.

8th Nightul, 1596 DR

Corad's come to me directly with a secret project! By Gond, it will be wonderous indeed - a golem imbued with the arcane woven so finely and so intricately that it could be said to be alive!

And this time, it'll be just him and me - no entourage, no arch mage, no aides. Some of this is for secrecy, I understand, but I can't help but wonder if he too sees the opportunity to spend time together.

Happy days, I am so excited!

4th Ches, 1598 DR

News has just come through; Emperor Nyx is slain. This news, while horrible and implies longterm instability throughout our region, has not dampened my spirits. Working with Corad has been a dream come true - I think there's a real connection between us, both professional and personal. I... I think he likes me. And I like him back.

4nd Mirtul, 1598 DR

We've been working constantly through the last three days, no sleep, right through the night. We're close, we're so close.

Last night, the machine stirred, and in a moment of euphoria... I kissed him. He kissed me back, too. Truly, this is the greatest time of my life - I can't imagine anything spoiling this!
