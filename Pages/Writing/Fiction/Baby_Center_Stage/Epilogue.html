Epilogue

Baby Center Stage - Chapter 9/9

Wren and Raven walked slowly through the park, admiring the full bloom of the trees at the height of spring. Birdsong filled the air, and squirrels played between the tombstones as they approached Jax’s marker.

 “It’s this one,” said Raven, pointing to a nondescript gray slab at the end of a row. They walked up, and Wren lay a flower in front of it. That movement startled a rabbit sleeping in its shade, which scampered off into the brush. The duo smiled, and chuckled lightly.

 They stood quietly for a few minutes, reflecting on the time they’d spent with the young woman. Raven sniffed, then knelt down, pulling a sharp rock out of their pocket. After quick glances left and right, they began scratching into the stone. “That’s better,” they said, blowing away the dust. “Jax Mateo. We will remember you as the lovely girl you were.”

 Caine conjured the circus’s signature theme, peppy and bouncy, just like always.

Gangle and Zooble and Kinger, too
Ragatha, Jax, and there’s Ribbit and Kaufmo
Day after day after day after day we fly
Past the moon and the sun and we don’t know why

 Suddenly a short jester woman popped onto the stage, horribly confused. “W-w-where am I?” She took a step back and bumped into a tall rabbit woman, knocking her back into her frog partner and causing a chain-reaction of stumbling that sent the entire circus to the floor.

 “Strike! Three points!” Yelled bubble.

 Jax stood and looked the jester over. “Caine, is this one of your NPCs, or is this a new sucker? ‘Cause if this is a new character, it’s Zooble’s turn to explain to her what the rest of her life is going to look like.” She flipped her long black hair over her shoulder and elbowed Ribbit. “Looks like Kaufmo’s sister, eh?”

 “Hey kid,” said Ribbit, walking over to the jester. “It’s going to be okay. We’ve got you.”
